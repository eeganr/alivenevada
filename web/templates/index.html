Let's Make Sure Nature's On Track, Shall We?

Alive Nevada is an initiative to protect the environment and the wildlife that thrives in it, which makes Nevada one of the best places to live on the planet.

Our goals are to:

  • help Nevadans learn about the biodiversity that make up the environment they live in and help protect them
  • help track the migratory path and behavior of animals fleeing ever more frequent wildfires
  • help protect our biodiversity from invasive species
  • create a collaborative, community based, crowd sourced database of observations of wildlife and invasive species distributions over the years

Why You Should Care:

The challenges facing Nevada’s environment, wildlife, grasslands and water bodies are immense and complicated. But we believe that the power of communities and collaboration can, and will, prove capable of meeting these challenges.

Northern Nevada is a beautiful place with pictureque views of the Sierra Nevada mountains, the meandering Truckee river, Lake Tahoe, Pyramid Lake, Washoe Lake, and an uncountable number of ponds, lakes and streams. This environment provides a safe habitat for many species of native wildlife and endangered species. These treasures are threatened by unprecedented challenges.

Nevada’s increasing average temperatures and increase in the frequency and intensity of heat waves is predicted to change the available habitat of many species and negatively impact the health of our wildlife. Given this prediction, it is the time to act now. By keeping a record of public observations of our wildlife, and providing the data in an open-source fashion for public science and/or lab research, we can understand the trend over years regarding the population, distribution, migration pathways, and survival ability of all observable species in our habitat. With trend data available, we can take proactive measures to intervene at appropriate times.

However, this can only be possible with the help of many Nevadans willing to record and report their observations for scientific research.

You can help by keeping a lookout for the presence of wildlife in your neighborhood, grasslands and water bodies. Create an account and simply log into the app to report your observation and upload a picture taken. If you cannot identify the animal, that is alright, enter unknown and upload a picture. We are in the process of building a AI (Artificial Intelligence) to identify the wildlife based on Nevada wildlife database.

If you need the crowdsourced data for your research, please contact us.
